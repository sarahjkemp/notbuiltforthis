The ADHD Admin Scripts

12 word-for-word scripts for the phone calls, emails and forms you've been avoiding.

When you're already overwhelmed, finding the right words can feel like one task too many. Phone calls, emails to authority figures, and forms with a lot riding on them can all make your brain go foggy right when you need it to work.

It's on its way.

Check your email — the PDF should land in under a minute. If it's not there in 5, check your spam folder and mark it "not spam" (this teaches your inbox to deliver future emails properly).

Where should I send it?

The PDF arrives in your inbox in under a minute. You'll also get the occasional email when I make something else useful. Unsubscribe any time.

Scripts solve this. You don't have to think. You read. The dentist receptionist does not care that you're reading from a script. They care that you booked the appointment.

This PDF has 12 of mine. Plus the 3-minute regulation protocol I do before every call, and what to do when the script isn't enough.

Doing this alone is the hard part

The scripts work. I've tested them on dentists, energy companies, HMRC, my landlord, my GP. They work because they remove cognitive load.

But here's the thing nobody talks about. Most ADHD adults don't fail because they don't have the right script. They fail because they're alone in the kitchen at 2pm trying to make the call and their nervous system is screaming and there's nobody there to say "now do it".

The script is necessary. It's not sufficient.

What actually works: body doubling

Body doubling is the science of co-regulation. When you're in the same space (physical or digital) as someone else who's regulated and doing the same kind of task, your nervous system can borrow theirs. The threat response shrinks. The script gets used.
