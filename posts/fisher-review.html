Fisher at Old Royal Naval College — moments before everything went wrong.

Last year, I went to 25 gigs. Most of them solo.

For a 40-year-old with ADHD, going alone isn’t sad — it’s tactical.

It removes decision fatigue. No checking if someone else is having fun. No emotional scanning.

Just me and the experience.

But this time, the system failed.

And when the system fails, everything collapses fast.

The setup

I paid £280 for a stage ticket.

Not for status. For control.

Space. Sightlines. Exit.

That’s what I call sensory insurance.

The collapse

Five minutes before Fisher came on, security removed us from the stage area.

The buffer was gone.

I was pushed into the crowd I had paid to avoid.

The moment

Within 60 seconds, a drunk person fell on me.

That was it.

System crash.

I left.

I didn’t see a single minute.

The verdict

The venue is beautiful.

The event management failed completely.

If you’re buying premium for sensory reasons, ask one thing:

Is the infrastructure fixed — or temporary?

If it’s temporary, it’s not insurance.

It’s a gamble.
